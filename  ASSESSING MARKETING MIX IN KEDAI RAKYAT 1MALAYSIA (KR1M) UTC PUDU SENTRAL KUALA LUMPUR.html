ASSESSING MARKETING MIX IN KEDAI RAKYAT 1MALAYSIA (KR1M), UTC PUDU CENTRAL,KUALA LUMPUR

BY

AINA HANNANI BINTI ZAINAL ABIDIN ,NUR AFIQAH BINTI AHMADI ,SITI HAWA BINTI ZUBER

                                                                                                                                ABSTRACT


Malaysia has embarked on developing program to promote its national brand trough the concept of buying goods at affordable price to the low  income and poor household in Malaysia. At present there are 85 KR1M stores across the country. However, the acceptance of KR1M from the community is rather low as compared to the other established retail stores. Amongst the problems are relatively not low-priced product, unpopular brand, unattractive packaging, incorrect target market and store layout. This study is conducted to explore the public OUTLOOK awareness towards KR1M and to identify the factors that attribute to the lower acceptance and market of KR1M business operation based on the 4Aâ€™s marketing mix concept, namely acceptability, affordability, accessibility and awareness. The total of 169 questionnaires has been distributed to KR1M customers in Klang Valley using random sampling. Results demonstrate that KR1M still unable to attract the customers because of non-competitive price, brand and packaging, as well as unattractive store image and layout. Impact of this study will contribute to the improvement plan for KR1M that will help the authorities to take appropriate actions to continuously enhance 1Malaysia campaign. local product. In 2011, Prime Minister has launched a 1Malaysia campaign that among the objectives is to promote buying local product by  establishing Kedai Rakyat 1Malaysia (KR1M). KR1M was introduced in order to provide common household.
